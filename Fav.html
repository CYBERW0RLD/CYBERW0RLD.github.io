Kris Dreemur

I like them not because they're the protagonist, but because of their personality that shines through in the tiniest details. It's charming in it's own way, despite their reputation as the local weird kid. They're just a kid who likes chocolate and pie, carries a knife around for comfort, and most importantly, they love their family. A LOT. Kris truly goes to show that no matter how weird they may seem, they're still a kid, and that you should never judge a book by its cover.

Ralsei

Look at him! He's just a little guy!! The skrimbly!!! Okay, in all seriousness, I love this guy. He's sweet and nice like a marshmallow. He brings comfort to my tired brain. During chapter 1, he was but a lonely prince who patiently waited for his friends to come, and because of that, he never really got to experience what friendship was like before. But now that he's on a journey with said friends, he's slowly starting to realize many things about not only his new friends, but himself as well. He's slowly learning his own identity, slowly learning that he's not just a Prince of the Dark, or one of the three fabled heroes that will save the world from darkness. He's much more than that, and carefully, he's discovering that as well, and I REALLY love that aspect of him.
